Definition

Gnosticism refers to several early religious movements (1st–4th century CE) asserting that salvation is achieved through gnosis — direct experiential knowledge of divine reality.

Core Principles

  • Salvation through knowledge, not faith alone
  • Radical distinction between spirit and matter
  • A transcendent, unknowable Source (Monad)
  • A lesser creator (Demiurge) governs the material cosmos
  • Divine spark imprisoned within humanity

Key Figures & Concepts

Valentinus

Second-century teacher who systematized Christian Gnostic theology.

Sophia

Divine Wisdom whose error initiates cosmic fragmentation.

Demiurge

Ignorant creator of the material world, ruler of the Archons.

Chronological Walkthrough (Primary Texts)

c. 50–100 CE | Gospel of Thomas
“The kingdom is inside you and it is outside you… When you know yourselves, you will be known.”

Jesus is portrayed as a revealer of self-knowledge. No crucifixion theology; salvation through awakening.

c. 100–130 CE | Apocryphon of John
“I am the invisible Spirit… It is not right to think of it as a god.”

Introduces the Monad, Aeons, and a transcendent divine source beyond the creator of the material world.

c. 120–150 CE | Sophia Myth
“Sophia desired to bring forth something by herself, without the consent of the Spirit.”

Explains cosmic disorder as the result of divine error, not moral evil.

c. 130–170 CE | Hypostasis of the Archons
“It is I who am God, and there is no other beside me.”

The Demiurge’s claim is framed as ignorance, not truth.

c. 150–180 CE | Gospel of Truth
“Ignorance of the Father brought anguish and terror, but knowledge appeared.”

Salvation is healing through knowledge, not forgiveness of sin.

c. 180–220 CE | Gospel of Philip
“Truth did not come into the world naked, but in types and images.”

Emphasis on symbols, sacraments, and mystical union.

1945 CE | Nag Hammadi Discovery

Original Gnostic texts recovered in Egypt, restoring first-hand sources.

Creation According to Gnostic Texts

1. The Monad
“The One is the invisible Spirit… It is impossible to describe.”

The true God does not create. It exists beyond being and thought.

2. Emanation of Aeons
“From the light came forth a power, perfect in glory.”

Reality unfolds through emanation, not intentional creation.

3. Sophia’s Error
“She brought forth something imperfect.”

Independent creation introduces deficiency into the divine order.

4. Birth of the Demiurge
“He did not know from where his power came.”

The Demiurge is ignorant of higher reality and believes himself supreme.

5. Material Cosmos
“They created the heavens and the earth according to their power.”

The world is ordered yet flawed, governed by Archons.

6. Humanity
“They fashioned Adam, but he lay motionless.”

Archons cannot create true life.

7. Divine Spark
“The breath of the Mother became power in him.”

Sophia implants divine light into humanity.

8. Purpose of Gnosis
“Ignorance is the mother of all evil.”

Salvation is awakening and return, not sacrifice or judgment.
